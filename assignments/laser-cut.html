HOLLOW SPHERE - TRUNCATED ICOSAHEDRON

Introduction

In geometry, the **truncated icosahedron** is a polyhedron that can be constructed by truncating all of the regular icosahedron's vertices. Intuitively, it may be regarded as **footballs (or soccer balls)** that are typically patterned with white hexagons and black pentagons.

It can be found in the application of geodesic dome structures such as those whose architecture Buckminster Fuller pioneered are often based on this structure. It is an example of an **Archimedean solid**, as well as a **Goldberg polyhedron**.

Other forms

There are many other methods and designs to create a sphere which are listed below

Hemisphere joining

This is a common industrial method. Two hemispherical shells are formed (by stamping, pressing, or molding) and then joined together.

Geodesic Sphere  - Segmented(used in this assignment - pentagons and hexagons)

We can create a geodesic sphere, similar to a geodesic dome, using triangular or hexagonal panels.

 Layered Construction

This involves cutting a series of concentric rings, each slightly smaller than the previous one and then stacking them accordingly gives a spherical shape.

Balls

This involves cutting and assembling curved surfaces to form a hollow sphere matching a design similar to that of balls.
